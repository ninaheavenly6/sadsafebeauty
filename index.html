About the Initiative

This platform aims to raise awareness regarding the importance of hygiene in cosmetic testing environments. It addresses risks associated with unsanitary tester usage and highlights the best practices for both retailers and consumers.

With proper education and sanitation protocols, users can significantly reduce the risk of skin and eye infections caused by contaminated cosmetic testers.

Hygiene Guidelines

For Retail Staff

  • Disinfect testers after each customer use
  • Replace testers every 2–3 months
  • Provide single-use applicators
  • Display visible hygiene guidelines
  • Dispose of expired or contaminated products
  • Make hand sanitizers readily available

For Customers

  • Wash or sanitize hands before testing
  • Use only disposable applicators
  • Avoid using testers on broken skin
  • Do not test products on eyes or mouth
  • Check for product expiration dates
  • Notify staff if testers appear unhygienic

High-Risk Cosmetic Products

Mascara

Moist environments can lead to bacterial growth near eyes

Lip Products

Oral contact increases transmission of infections

Eye Products

Sensitive areas prone to irritation and bacterial transfer

Cream Products

Prone to contamination from repeated dipping
